Helix72 TAM Structural Analyzer
Tri-Antagonist Matrix™ Motion & Structural Analysis System
Abstract

The Helix72 TAM Structural Analyzer is a deterministic biomechanical analysis system designed to evaluate human posture, motion, and structural stability using vector-based modeling. By integrating the Tri-Antagonist Matrix™ (TAM) with the Helix72 stability framework, the system provides a structured method for identifying biomechanical imbalance, compensation patterns, and kinetic chain behavior.

The system converts visual skeletal data into measurable outputs that are reproducible, interpretable, and applicable across clinical and performance environments.

1. Introduction

Understanding human movement requires more than isolated joint analysis. Traditional approaches often evaluate segments independently, missing how forces propagate across the body as a system.

The Helix72 TAM Structural Analyzer addresses this by modeling the body as a connected structural network, where load, motion, and compensation are continuously exchanged across the kinetic chain.

This system was developed to provide:

A unified model of structure and motion

Real-time interpretation of biomechanical behavior

A consistent framework for cross-disciplinary use

2. System Foundation
2.1 Tri-Antagonist Matrix™ (TAM)

The TAM model defines four functional roles within the biomechanical system:

Agonist (Driver) – the dominant force producing motion or load

Antagonist (Counterforce) – the opposing force regulating movement

Bi-Antagonist (Stabilizer System) – the paired system maintaining balance

Tri-Antagonist (Anchor / Saboteur) – the node where structural locking or failure occurs

This model allows the system to identify not only where imbalance exists, but how it behaves and propagates.

2.2 Helix72 Stability Framework

The Helix72 engine classifies structural integrity into four discrete states:

STABLE – balanced and controlled structure

COMPENSATION – adaptive redistribution of load

STRESS – increasing mechanical strain

CASCADE – multi-chain structural failure

These states provide a clear, standardized interpretation of biomechanical condition.

3. System Architecture

The Helix72 TAM analyzer operates through a four-layer structure:

3.1 Input Layer

Captures motion and posture data:

Camera-based skeletal tracking

Landmark detection

Vector mapping of joints and segments

3.2 Geometry & Metrics Layer

Transforms positional data into measurable outputs:

Joint angles

Alignment offsets

Center-of-mass deviation

Symmetry and corridor analysis

Foot and ankle orientation (current model under refinement)

3.3 Structural Analysis Layer

Applies TAM and Helix72 logic:

Chain integrity scoring

Burden distribution mapping

Origin node detection

Propagation pathway tracking

Stability classification

3.4 Output Layer

Produces interpretable results:

Structural summaries

TAM role assignments

SOAP-style clinical reports

Pre/post session comparisons

4. Core Analytical Functions

The system evaluates biomechanical behavior through:

Chain Integrity – overall structural coherence

Center-of-Mass Positioning – balance and control

Load Propagation – how force travels through the body

Compensation Detection – adaptive movement patterns

Structural Symmetry – left/right balance

These functions enable identification of both primary dysfunction and secondary compensation.

7. Known System Limitation (Current Build)

The current version includes a known limitation within the foot and ankle vector calculation layer.

Affected measurements include:

Ankle alignment

Foot orientation

Stance symmetry

Certain center-of-mass projections

These values may appear overly sensitive and should be interpreted as preliminary indicators.

Upper-chain measurements (pelvis, spine, shoulders, torso) remain reliable.

A corrected vector model is scheduled for the next system update.
